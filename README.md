🚗 Uber Data Analysis & Insights
🚀 Project Overview

This project focuses on data cleaning and Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of Uber trip data to uncover patterns in passenger behavior, trip purposes, and driver performance.

The objective is to derive actionable business insights that can help improve operational efficiency, customer experience, and strategic decision-making.

🎯 Objectives
Understand trip distribution and demand patterns
Analyze user behavior and trip purposes
Evaluate driver efficiency and speed trends
Generate data-driven business recommendations
🛠️ Data Cleaning & Preprocessing

Before performing analysis, the dataset was cleaned and transformed to ensure accuracy and consistency:

🔹 Data Normalization
Standardized inconsistent location names (e.g., “Karachi”, “New York”) using mapping techniques
🔹 Data Type Conversion
Converted Start Date and End Date from object to datetime format

🔹 Feature Engineering
⏱️ Calculated trip duration (in minutes)
🚗 Derived average speed per trip
🌅 Created Time of Day segments:
Morning
Afternoon
Evening
Night
📏 Categorized trips into:
Short
Medium
Long

📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
🔍 Trip Distribution
Majority of trips fall within the 10–20 mile range
Indicates strong demand for short-to-medium urban travel

⏰ Peak Demand Analysis
Highest ride volume observed during:
Night (8 PM – 5 AM)
Afternoon
Suggests need for higher driver availability during these periods
📌 Trip Purpose & Category
~99% of trips are Business-related
Top contributing purposes:
Meeting
Meal/Entertain


🚗 Driver Behavior Analysis
Drivers tend to maintain higher speeds during:
Meeting trips
Temporary Site visits
Indicates urgency-driven driving behavior
📏 Trip Length Analysis
Business trips show:
More long-distance outliers
Personal trips are generally shorter and more consistent


📈 Visualizations

The project includes the following visual insights:

📊 Histogram → Trip distance distribution

⏰ Time-based Analysis → Peak demand patterns
💡 Executive Summary & Recommendations


📌 Conclusion
The dataset is heavily dominated by business users (~99%)
Peak demand occurs during:
Night hours
Afternoon
Most trips are medium-distance (10–20 miles)


🚀 Recommendations
👨‍✈️ Strategic Staffing
Increase driver availability between 8 PM – 5 AM to meet peak demand
🎯 Targeted Marketing
Focus on corporate clients, as they form the majority of users
⚠️ Safety Monitoring
Implement speed alerts for “Meeting” trips, where higher speeds are observed
📅 Seasonal Planning
Prepare for increased demand in November–December, especially for medium-distance trips
